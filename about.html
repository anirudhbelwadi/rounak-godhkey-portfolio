ABOUT

I am a first-year MS-AECM student at Carnegie Mellon University with a strong interest in pursuing practical experience in construction and design-build. With 3.5 years of professional experience across architecture, construction, and real estate development in India, I bring a multidisciplinary understanding of how projects move from concept to execution.

My architectural background has trained me to balance spatial efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and constructability, while collaborating closely with clients and consultants. At CMU, I am expanding this foundation through advanced coursework in project scheduling, workflow optimization, and integrated project delivery, skills that strengthen my ability to work within complex design-build environments.

In addition, my exposure to project management has given me insight into the full project lifecycle, including land acquisition, regulatory approvals, brand coordination, and contractor management. I played a key role in developing highway rest stops in India, working with multinational brands such as McDonald's and KFC, which helped refine my abilities in negotiation, documentation, and stakeholder coordination.

I am passionate about bridging the gap between design and construction to deliver efficient, well-coordinated projects. I am eager to gain hands-on experience with a firm like Kitchell that values collaboration, technical excellence, and community impact.
